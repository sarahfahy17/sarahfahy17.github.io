HISTORY
Opened in 1940, the Rivoli Cinemas is an Art Deco inspired architectural masterpiece. Designed by the famous cinema architects Taylor and Soilleux, the Rivoli Cinema's style is a combination of Streamlined Moderne and Jazz Moderne. In 1968, the original cinema was divided into two cinemas, forming the Rivoli Twin. It now contains eight cinemas and seats 1550 people.
The current cinema replaced the Rivoli Theatre, which was located at 570 Burke Road and was opened in 1921.
